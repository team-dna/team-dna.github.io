What we heard

Our understanding of the problem

We spoke with Steffan and Dean from Immigration NZ about the Employer Accreditation process, and discovered several pain points that affect both applicants and Immigration NZ.

Paper Based

Applications for Employer Accreditation are paper based. The paperwork must be transported by mail between the employer and different Government offices around the country, handed over to administration, and filed and ordered by staff. This results in unnecessary handling of applications before they’ve been reviewed.

Resource Required

The job of filling in an application or renewal is given to an HR employee, or sometimes a lawyer or consultant. It requires considerable time to complete and thus is an opportunity cost. This cost is a barrier to entry for many SMEs that would otherwise benefit from being accredited.

Errors

Many applications contain errors and must be returned (about 70% of first-time applications, and 30% of renewals), further adding to the resource cost and time delay.

Transparency

The status of a business’s application is not made visible to them:

  • If their application requires changes or more information, they are only made aware of this when their application is returned by mail.
  • Not all businesses are sent reminders of their impending renewal deadline.
  • Some businesses use OIA requests to gather the information they had sent last year because they don’t keep a copy.
  • Some businesses forget (due to high HR churn) that they are accredited.

Our thoughts

How we can apply digital solutions to these pain points

Transparent Allow businesses to access the status and content of their accreditation application, through the use of a digital dashboard. Therefore, they will know when they will be accredited, what additional documentation they need to provide to Immigration NZ, and when they will need to renew their accreditation.
Fast Ensure that applications are processed significantly faster with the use of digital solutions. For example, making documentation digital will remove the reliance on mailing and storing physical documents, making access easier for both Immigration NZ and accredited businesses.
Flexible Different industries have different needs. Customising the application process depending on the size and type of business could significantly reduce the amount of paperwork required to become successfully accredited.
